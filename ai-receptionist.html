Section 01 · The Cost

The most expensive call is the one nobody answers

The average small business misses a meaningful share of its inbound calls — after hours, on the other line, mid-job. Most of those callers never leave a voicemail. They scroll down to the next name on Google and call your competitor instead. It never becomes a lead, so it never shows up on a single report — which is exactly why it goes unfixed for years.

01

The 6:40pm call

It rings four times after you've closed and rolls to voicemail. No message. That caller is now your competitor's customer — and you'll never know it happened.

02

The on-the-other-line call

You're already on a call or on a job. The second caller doesn't wait — they hang up and dial the next listing. An AI receptionist answers both at once.

03

The math nobody runs

Calls missed per week, times the share that never call back, times what one customer is worth. Run it once and the monthly cost of an AI receptionist looks like a rounding error.

Section 03 · The Honest Comparison

Voicemail vs. answering service vs. AI receptionist

Every call you can't take goes through one of three doors. Here's the straight version — including where the AI loses.

Option 01

Voicemail

Free, and in 2026 the same as no door at all. Most callers don't leave a message — they leave you. The default that quietly costs the most.

Option 02

Human answering service

A real person, but a per-minute meter, generic scripts, after-hours upcharges, and they take a message rather than booking the job. Better than voicemail — until the invoice arrives.

Option 03

AI receptionist

Answers instantly 24/7, books directly, logs to your CRM, flat predictable cost, trained on your business — and callable right now. Loses only on calls that need real human judgment, which it hands to you.
