Case Study · Dog Gone Spoiled

From no website to indexed local visibility.

Dog Gone Spoiled in Riverside, Rhode Island started from the hardest possible baseline: no website at all. We built the domain, the local search foundation, the content surface, and the AI-readiness layer from zero.

The Baseline

A loved local business with no crawlable home base

Before the engagement, Dog Gone Spoiled did not have a website. The business had local demand and word-of-mouth trust, but Google and AI engines had little structured material to crawl, understand, cite, or rank.

The job was not a redesign. It was a first build: domain, site architecture, local service content, schema, Google Business Profile support, and campaign assets that could give the business a durable online surface.

The Build

Website, local SEO, and AI visibility in one pass

  • Website from zero: doggonespoiled.com was built with service content, crawlable HTML, metadata, and schema.
  • Local search foundation: Google Business Profile setup and local relevance work gave search engines a consistent entity to trust.
  • Content and creative: video ad creative and business-specific copy turned the site into more than a placeholder.
  • Technical quality: mobile Lighthouse verification on June 10, 2026 showed SEO 100, Best Practices 96, Accessibility 81, and Performance 70.

The Result

From zero presence to measurable search surface

Semrush now reports 14 organic keywords for a site that did not exist before the engagement. The strongest current positions include "spoiled riverside" at #7, "dog grooming providence" at #22, and "dog groomers providence ri" at #42.

For a new local-business domain, those rankings matter because they prove the foundation is indexable, relevant, and already competing beyond the brand name. The next job is compounding: reviews, fresh posts, more service content, and stronger local links.

What This Proves

A local business does not need to wait months to become understandable to search engines. If the website, profile, content, and technical foundation are built together, visibility starts with a real baseline instead of guesswork.
