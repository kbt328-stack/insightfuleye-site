Case Study · Fisher's Fruit Farm

A farm rebuild with 105 organic keywords.

Fisher's Fruit & Christmas Tree Farm had real seasonal demand and a real family business behind it. The assignment was to make the website perform like a durable search asset, not a brochure.

The Baseline

A seasonal farm needed search coverage that matched demand

Fisher's Fruit Farm is not one simple keyword. It is Christmas trees, fruit trees, cherry picking, local farm visits, and seasonal demand around Rochester and Brockport, New York.

That means the site had to give search engines clear pages, clear entities, and clear language for multiple search paths instead of relying on one generic farm homepage to do every job.

The Build

Technical foundation, analytics, and seasonal search intent

  • New website: fast, crawlable pages built around the farm's real products and seasonal demand.
  • Analytics foundation: visits, calls, and inquiries tracked so campaigns can be judged by source and outcome.
  • Google Business Profile management: local presence maintained around seasonal farm activity.
  • B2B outreach: chef and cidery outreach supported with tested messaging and measurable response paths.
  • Technical quality: mobile Lighthouse verification on June 10, 2026 showed SEO 100, Accessibility 98, Best Practices 96, and Performance 85.

The Result

105 keywords, with 38 on pages one or two

Semrush now reports 105 organic keywords for fishersfruit.com, including 38 on pages one or two. Current ranking examples include "fisher christmas trees" at #4, "fisher tree farm" at #5, "fruit tree farm" at #18, "tree farm rochester ny" at #19, and "cherry picking rochester ny" at #21.

The important pattern is breadth. The site is not only ranking for one brand query. It is earning visibility across seasonal and category searches that match how customers actually look for the farm.

What This Proves

Local SEO works best when the site mirrors the real business: products, seasons, locations, and customer intent. For Fisher's Fruit Farm, that meant building a search surface wide enough for Christmas trees, fruit, picking, and regional farm discovery.
